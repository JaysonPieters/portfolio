Optimized navmesh calculations

Because the monster is chasing the player it needs to update alot, because the player also moves around. While making the game, i eventually noticed the map became so large the navmesh calculation took longer to process than it actually moves. To combat this i made a small piece of code that worked wonders. In total I spent a few days making sure the navmesh reached everywhere.

Here are some of the key feature.

The original idea

At first I had it as simple as a simple script that updates the location of where the navmesh needs to move every half a second or so. However because this waypoint gets updated so often the navmesh needs to recalculate everything, and at further distances on the map this took significantly longer than the 0.5 seconds. As a result, it didn't move at all.

The new idea

Because the navmesh agent didn't move at all, I hade to come up with something new. So instead of updating every 0.5 seconds, I opted to make this a dynamic value: The further away the monster is from the player, the less it updates its path and vise versa.
